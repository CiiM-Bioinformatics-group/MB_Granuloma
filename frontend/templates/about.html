About stRNAAtlas

Welcome to the Mycobacterial Granuloma Spatial Atlas, an interactive web platform designed to explore the spatial organization and molecular dynamics of granulomas induced by mycobacterial infections. Utilizing state-of-the-art spatial transcriptomics, our comprehensive atlas reveals distinct granuloma niches—each characterized by specialized immune cells, signaling pathways, and tissue remodeling processes. Our dataset encompasses a diverse array of granulomas from tuberculosis (TB), non-tuberculous mycobacterial (NTM) infections, lymphoid tissues, and sarcoidosis, highlighting unique features that differentiate infectious from non-infectious granulomatous diseases. This webtool allows researchers to:


- Navigate spatially resolved transcriptomic data from human granuloma tissues.
- Investigate cellular composition and niche-specific gene expression patterns.
- Examine detailed pathway and cytokine activity across granuloma niches.
- Compare granuloma types to identify disease-specific biomarkers and therapeutic targets.


Our atlas offers a valuable resource for biomedical researchers, clinicians, and immunologists aiming to understand the complex interplay of immune responses, pathogen persistence, and tissue remodeling in granulomatous diseases. Start exploring to gain new insights into granuloma biology and potential avenues for precision therapeutic interventions.
