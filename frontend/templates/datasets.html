Overview of the Study Workflow

Schematic overview of the study workflow. Biobanked lung tissue samples were collected from a discovery cohort (n=33) and a validation cohort (n=6). The samples were processed using the Visium probe-based spatial transcriptomics (10x genomics). After H&E staining and cDNA library preparation, sequencing was performed to map spatial gene expression in granulomas. Spatial niches were identified using clustering and cell-type deconvolution methods, which were improved by integration of the Lung v2 dataset, leading to insights into the functional roles of each niche and comparison with sarcoid granulomas. Main findings from spatial transcriptomics were validated through immunohistofluorescence protein stainings.

Summary of the Main Findings in this Study

Granulomas are structured immune environments central to Mycobacterium infections. Using spatial transcriptomics on 39 patient samples, we identified five distinct niches within lymphoid and pulmonary granulomas. MTB granulomas show strong JAK-STAT and IFN-lambda signaling, while NTM granulomas exhibit elevated hypoxia. The findings reveal spatially organized immune responses and distinct disease-specific signatures.
